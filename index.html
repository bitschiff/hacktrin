About Us

HackTrin is Trinity School’s fifth annual hackathon, run in partnership with The Spence School. Everyone from beginners to experts can join, and we are open to middle schoolers and high schoolers. You can form teams of 1-4 students, even across different schools. We will announce the surprise theme at our Wednesday night kickoff event the week of HackTrin, after which you can start coding, and continue until the end of the Sunday, February 25 event, when you’ll present your projects to our panels of judges to win prizes in multiple categories (including a beginner category)!

FAQ


What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is an event where teams get together and build a project relating to coding, using software or hardware components, in a short period of time.

What types of projects are allowed at HackTrin V?

Anything you want, as long as it involves some coding component. Popular projects in the past have been websites, apps, games, gagdets, or whatever else you come up with!

Are teacher chaperones required?

Yes, for insurance purposes, Trinity requires one teacher chaperone per school. Please email us if that's an issue for you!

Is there a theme at HackTrin V?

We're announcing the theme for HackTrin V at the Wednesday kickoff event, as well as notifying all participants by email.

What's the Wednesday kickoff event?

On Wednesday, February 21, from 6:00-7:30, we're having a optional pre-hackathon event where we reveal the theme (we'll also email everyone the theme), give information about Sunday, and host some workshops! We'll provide dinner at the event. After the kickoff event, coding can begin!.

Who can come to HackTrin V?

HackTrin V is open to any current high school or middle school students. Middle school students will compete in a special division, HackTrin Junior.

Will there be food at HackTrin V?

Yes, we will provide breakfast and lunch, as well as snacks throughout the day. We will have options for any dietary restrictions also!
